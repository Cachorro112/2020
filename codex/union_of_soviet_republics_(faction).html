Union of Soviet Republics (faction)

The Union of Soviet Republics rose from the ashes of the socially collapsed Soviet Union in 1994, marking a new era in its history. While retaining the socialist roots of its predecessor, this nation embarked on a path of gradual change, embracing elements of cultural Westernization and liberalization of the economy. Unlike the rigid central planning of the past, the Union now allows for some local enterprises to thrive, particularly in agriculture, fostering innovation and diversity in production. Perhaps the most significant shift is towards a semi-democratic system, where citizens have the right to vote for parliamentarians representing the Soviet Party.
